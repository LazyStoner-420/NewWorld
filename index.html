Comprehensive Lawn Care & Landscape Solutions · Kingston & Surrounding Areas, Ontario

Full-Service Outdoor Care

Everything Your Property Needs

New World Lawn Care offers a comprehensive suite of outdoor services for residential and commercial properties throughout Kingston and the surrounding region. From routine weekly maintenance to full landscape installations, our programmes are designed around Ontario's distinct four-season climate and cool-season grass varieties.

All services are performed by trained, insured professionals using commercial-grade equipment. We operate in full compliance with the Ontario Pesticides Act, the Nutrient Management Act, and all applicable municipal bylaws.

Pricing shown is indicative and based on standard lot sizes in the Kingston region. Your final quote will be calculated following a free on-site assessment.

✂️Lawn MaintenanceYear-Round

Mowing & Edging

Precision cuts following the one-third rule for Ontario cool-season grasses (Kentucky bluegrass, fescues, ryegrass). Rotated mowing patterns to prevent soil compaction. Sharp blade cuts — never torn. Crisp string-trimming and edging of all borders and beds.

Starting from$45 – $75 / visit

Lawn Rolling

Spring lawn rolling to address frost heave and smooth uneven surfaces following Ontario's freeze-thaw cycles. Applied only when soil moisture is appropriate to avoid compaction — a distinction few services observe.

Starting from$75 – $150 / visit

Dethatching

Mechanical removal of thatch buildup exceeding ½ inch, which impedes water and nutrient penetration. Best performed in early spring or early fall in Ontario's climate for optimal turf recovery.

Starting from$120 – $250
🌱Turf Health ProgramsSeasonal Programs

Core Aeration

Hollow-tine core aeration to relieve soil compaction, improve root depth, and enhance water absorption. Recommended annually in Ontario — ideally mid-August through September for cool-season grasses when soil is moist but not saturated.

Starting from$80 – $180

Overseeding

Premium Ontario-adapted seed blends broadcast immediately following aeration for optimal seed-to-soil contact. We select varieties rated for Kingston's hardiness zone (5b/6a) — disease-resistant, drought-tolerant, and dense.

Starting from$80 – $160

Fertilization Program

Four-step seasonal fertilization following Ontario's Nutrient Management Act timing restrictions. No applications before April 1 or after November 15. Soil-pH tested blends with controlled-release nitrogen — no phosphorus applications to established lawns per Ontario regulation.

Starting from$55 – $100 / application

Topdressing

Application of a fine compost or sand-compost blend to level surface irregularities, improve soil biology, and stimulate microbial activity. An increasingly popular organic alternative to synthetic inputs.

Starting from$150 – $350

Weed Control

Compliant with Ontario's Cosmetic Pesticides Ban Act. We use Class 11 exempt products (acetic acid, iron-based formulations) for broadleaf weeds, combined with cultural practices — proper mowing height, aeration, and overseeding — which are the most effective long-term weed suppression strategies.

Starting from$60 – $130 / treatment

Grub & Pest Management

White grub monitoring and treatment using nematode applications (exempt under Ontario legislation). Nematodes are most effective when applied in late July to early August when soil temperature exceeds 15°C — precise timing is critical and is something we track carefully.

Starting from$90 – $180
💧Irrigation ServicesSpring – Fall

System Installation

Design and installation of underground irrigation systems with rain sensors and smart controllers. All work performed to Ontario Building Code requirements and municipal water authority standards, including backflow prevention device installation.

Starting from$2,800 – $6,500

Spring Start-Up

Full system activation following Ontario's frost season: zone-by-zone inspection, head adjustment, controller programming, and backflow preventer check. Typically performed mid-May in the Kingston area after last frost risk has passed.

Starting from$80 – $140

Fall Winterization

Compressed-air blow-out of all zones prior to Ontario's freeze season to prevent pipe and head damage. Recommended by mid-October in the Kingston area. Includes controller shut-down and insulation of above-ground components.

Starting from$90 – $150
🍂Seasonal CleanupSpring & Fall

Spring Cleanup

Comprehensive property preparation following Ontario's winter: debris and leaf removal, bed edging and reshaping, shrub and perennial pruning, winter kill assessment, lawn raking, and first fertilizer application. Typically scheduled April through May.

Starting from$200 – $600

Fall Cleanup

End-of-season property preparation: leaf collection and removal, cutting back perennials, winterizing tender plants, final mowing at a reduced height (2.5–3 inches for Ontario cool-season grass), and mulching of beds to protect against frost heave.

Starting from$200 – $600

Leaf Removal

Stand-alone leaf collection service throughout October and November. We vacuum, bag, and remove all organic material — or mulch in place where appropriate. Compliant with municipal composting programs in Kingston and area.

Starting from$80 – $250 / visit
🌳Landscaping & DesignCustom Quote

Landscape Design

Full-scope design services from planting plans to hardscape integration. We work with Ontario-hardy species rated for Zone 6a/6b, native plants preferred where appropriate. Design consultations include CAD-rendered layouts and plant lists with supply pricing.

Starting from$350 – $900 (design)

Planting & Bed Installation

Supply and installation of trees, shrubs, perennials, and annuals. All material guaranteed for one full growing season. We source from certified Ontario nurseries and prioritise low-maintenance, regionally appropriate plant selections.

Starting fromCustom Quote

Mulching

Application of premium hardwood or cedar mulch (3-inch depth) to suppress weeds, retain moisture, and improve bed aesthetics. All beds edged clean prior to application. We use mulch that meets the Compost Quality Alliance standard.

Starting from$60 – $90 / yard installed

Interlock & Hardscape

Walkways, patios, retaining walls, and steps using Unilock or equivalent approved products. All base preparation meets Ontario's frost-depth requirements. We obtain necessary municipal permits for structures where required.

Starting fromCustom Quote

Sod Installation

Supply and installation of fresh-cut Ontario sod. Includes grade preparation, starter fertilizer application, and installation. Sod sourced from local Ontario growers — typically delivered and installed same-day for maximum freshness.

Starting from$1.20 – $1.80 / sq ft

Tree & Shrub Care

Formative pruning, deadwood removal, and shaping for ornamental trees and shrubs. All pruning follows ISA (International Society of Arboriculture) guidelines. We do not perform hazard tree removal — a separate arborist service is recommended for large removals.

Starting from$85 – $250 / session
❄️Snow & Ice ManagementNov – Apr

Residential Snow Clearing

Driveway and walkway clearing triggered at 5cm accumulation. Service window: within 4 hours of snowfall ending. We use rubber-edged plows and padded shovels to protect paving surfaces. Salt application compliant with municipal guidelines to protect local water bodies.

Starting from$350 – $700 / season

Commercial Snow Management

Lot clearing, salting, and ice control for commercial properties. Includes site-specific snow management plans, priority scheduling, and liability documentation. Contracts include 24/7 emergency contact during weather events.

Starting fromCustom Contract

De-Icing & Sand

Application of calcium chloride, magnesium chloride, or sand blends to walkways and entries. We use the lowest effective rate to minimise environmental impact — in accordance with the Ontario Road Salt Management guidelines and municipal salt management plans.

Starting from$30 – $80 / application

Our Story

Built on Craftsmanship & Community

New World Lawn Care was founded in 2015 by James Novak, a horticulture professional who built his early career with regional landscape firms in Eastern Ontario before striking out on his own. What began as a one-truck operation serving a handful of Kingston properties has grown into a full-service team — all locally hired, all professionally trained.

The name "New World" reflects our belief that every property — no matter its current condition — holds the potential to become something extraordinary. We do not simply maintain lawns — we cultivate outdoor spaces that bring genuine joy and lasting value to the people who live in them.

Our growth has been driven almost entirely by word-of-mouth referrals. We have never run a discount promotion, because we believe that quality is its own advertisement. Today we serve over 600 residential and commercial properties across Kingston and the surrounding region, and we still answer our own phones.
